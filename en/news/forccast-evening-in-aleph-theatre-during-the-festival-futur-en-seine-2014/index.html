FORCCAST evening in Aleph theatre during the festival Futur en Seine 2014

Published by Equipe FORCCAST on 02 Oct 2014

Filed under Events

On June 18th during the festival Futur en Seine, students of Sciences Po, Télécom ParisTech and Aleph’s actors presented their work on mapping controversies by performing sketches directed by Oscar Castro.

The students of Télécom ParisTech dramatized and played two controversies on prostitution, whereas those performed by the students of Sciences Po were dedicated to dyslexia and meat consumption.

This experience – new for some – allowed highlighting the role of theatre as a tool for the exploration of controversies.

At the end of this event, students were able to debate and communicate on the different theatrical processes, the possible alteration of opinions on the subject of dramatization and the theatre as a new way of publishing controversies.
